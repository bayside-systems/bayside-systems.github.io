Disposable Identity Platform

Prove you’re human. Say nothing else.

undentity provides 30-day online identities that confirm you’re a real person in a geographic region — nothing more. No history. No tracking. No personal data beyond what’s necessary to verify you’re human.

The Problem

Online platforms need to verify users are human, not bots or bot farms. But traditional verification requires revealing too much personal information: your name, email, phone number, browsing history, and more. This creates privacy risks and enables tracking across platforms.

The undentity Solution

How it works:

  1. Obtain an identity: Visit a participating location (like a grocery store or Redbox kiosk)
  2. Verification: Prove you’re human without revealing personal details
  3. 30-day access: Use your identity for online platforms where you need human verification
  4. Privacy by default: The identity reveals nothing about who you are, only that you’re human in a region

Use Cases

Pseudo-Anonymity Platforms
For forums, discussion boards, and platforms where you want to participate without permanent tracking or doxxing risks.

Bot Prevention
For services that need human verification but don’t require personal identity information.

Privacy-First Communication
For secure messaging or communication platforms where you want to prove you’re human without compromising privacy.

Research & Testing
For researchers and developers testing bot detection systems or studying online behavior without personal data leakage.

Technical Details

  • 30-day lifespans: Each identity expires after 30 days, preventing long-term tracking
  • Geographic scope: Identifiers are region-specific (e.g., “human in Virginia Beach metro area”)
  • No personal data: The system doesn’t store your name, email, phone, or other PII
  • Simple verification: QR codes or NFC at participating locations for quick identity pickup

Defense & Government

For defense researchers and government contractors who need to:

  • Participate in open-source discussions without linking to personal identities
  • Conduct threat research while maintaining operational security
  • Engage in community forums without permanent digital footprints
